Reinstatement

Reapplication Process for BLS Practitioners with Expired EMS Certification

To get an expired Pennsylvania BLS certification reinstated...

Either... ...or...

Submit the following to EMSI:

  1. Proof of previous PA certification as an FR or an EMT.

    This may be a copy of a PA DOH certificate and/or copy of wallet card.

  2. Proof of current CPR certification.

  3. Proof of successful completion of recertification requirements within a 36-month period prior to the date of application.
    • EMT requires 24 total hours with at least 12 CE in M/T category
    • FR requires 16 total hours with at least 8 CE in M/T category
  4. A completed Student Application OpScan™ form.

    • Not available online.  (However, see a completed sample here:  Student Application Opscan Form.)

    • Complete after all the above requirements are met.

    • Follow the Instructions for Completing Student Application Form except at instruction step 6, Special Codes, Column A - G, proceed as follows:

      • In columns A and B, enter the last two digits of the current year.

      • In columns C through G, proceed as follows:

     

    SPECIAL CODES
    C D E F G ...
    FRs, enter:  0 2 0 0 0 ...
     
     

    SPECIAL CODES
    C D E F G ...
    EMTs, enter:  7 1 0 0 0 ...
  5. Complete the Criminal History Reporting Form.

Submit the above to:

Emergency Medical Service Institute
221-2500 Penn Avenue
Pittsburgh PA 15221
ATTN:  Reinstatement Application
 Challenge the state written and practical certification examinations.

Call EMSI to get exam dates and to register for exams.

On exam day, you must bring:

  1. Proof of previous PA certification
  2. Proof of current CPR certification
  3. Photo ID
