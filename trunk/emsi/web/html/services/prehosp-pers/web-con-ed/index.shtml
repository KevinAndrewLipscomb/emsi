Web-based Con Ed

»

 Some (not all) of the classes on the commonwealth's CentreLearn Learning Management System (LMS) are approved for Con Ed.  The LMS is free for Pennsylvania EMS practitioners. LMS

»

Pennsylvania recognizes the EMInet line of Internet-based continuing education courses.  To receive credit for EMInet course completions, practioners must use the Continuing Education By Endorsement process.

Warning!  Read This!

  1. EMInet is not the same as EMSI.  EMSI is a nonprofit regional council.  EMInet is a line of commercial online training products.

  2. EMInet is offered by a company called HealthStream.  To use EMInet, you must establish an account with HealthStream.

  3. Once you establish an account with HealthStream, you should go to EMInet.com for all your training.  Do not take courses branded "HealthStream EMS".  They are not yet suitable for use in Pennsylvania.

  4. The credit hours listed for an EMInet course are often split between the Medical/Trauma category and the Other category.  To find out how they are split, you should look the course number up in EMSAMS.

  5. You must submit a Continuing Education By Endorsement form to EMSI for each EMInet course that you complete.

If you don't understand this, call EMSI.

NOTE:  Pennsylvania does not recognize Internet-based con-ed from myWebCE.com nor from any other Internet-based source than the LMS and EMInet.

For more information about obtaining con ed using EMInet, visit their web site and contact the EMSI Regional Education Coordinator.
